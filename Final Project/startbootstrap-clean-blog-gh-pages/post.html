Species and Threats

Why Should You Care

Currently, whales are classified as endangered on the Endangered Species Act and the International Union for Conservation of Nature's (IUCN) Red List of Threatened Species. Whales are at the top of the food chain when it comes to the ocean. They have a large impact on the overall health of the marine environment. According to the World Wide Fun for Nature, six out of the thirteen great whales species are endangered.

Threats

There are several factors contributing to the dwindling numbers of whales on the earth: industry, commercial whaling, and climate change.

The Industry is a threat to these creatures because whales are hit by large ships and become trapped in fishing equipment. Specifically, shipping activity and oil and gas development can cause the whales to lose their hearing. This then restricts whales from locating food sources and breeding grounds.

Commercial whaling kills over 1000 whales.

Climate Change affect the whales by lowering the amount of their food sources. Thus, whales have to migrate further to find food. This shift in the climate has begun to hurt the reproductive rates of certain species such as the North Atlantic Right whale.
